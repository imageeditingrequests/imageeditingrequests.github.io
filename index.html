Abstract

Generative AI holds significant promise for automating everyday image modification tasks. However, its effectiveness in addressing real-world editing requests has not been thoroughly investigated. To bridge this gap, we introduce a large-scale dataset capturing a wide range of real-world image editing tasks. Our dataset comprises 83k unique requestsâ€”including 71k single-image modifications and 12k multi-image tasksâ€”which together yield 305k source-edited image pairs. We systematically categorize these requests by action types, subject matter, and creativity levels to enable a detailed analysis of their characteristics. In addition, we evaluate 50 AI-based image editing models by testing them on 328 diverse requests, resulting in 1,654 distinct AI-generated edits. Human evaluators compared these AI edits with human-produced edits, revealing that although human edits are strongly preferred, AI models can successfully handle approximately one-third of the most common requests.
